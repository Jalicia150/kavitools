# How to Create a Professional Invoice

Getting paid as a freelancer or small business owner starts with sending a professional invoice. A clean, well-structured invoice not only looks credible — it reduces payment delays and disputes.

Here's exactly what you need to include and how to create one in under 60 seconds.

## What Every Invoice Should Include

### 1. Your Business Information
Include your full business name (or your legal name if you're a sole proprietor), address, email, and phone number. This establishes credibility and gives clients a way to reach you about payments.

### 2. Client Information
Always include your client's name, company name, and address. This prevents confusion when the invoice reaches their accounts payable department.

### 3. Invoice Number
Use a consistent numbering system. A common format is `INV-YYMM-XXXX` (e.g., INV-2604-0001). Sequential numbers help both you and your client track payments.

### 4. Dates
Two dates matter:
- **Issue date** — when you sent the invoice
- **Due date** — when payment is expected (Net 15, Net 30, or a specific date)

### 5. Line Items with Descriptions
Break down your work into clear line items. Each should include:
- A description of the service or product
- Quantity
- Rate (hourly, per unit, or flat fee)
- Line total

Vague descriptions like "consulting" lead to questions and delays. Be specific: "Website redesign — homepage and 3 landing pages" is much better.

### 6. Totals
Show the subtotal, any applicable tax, and the grand total prominently. Make the amount due impossible to miss.

### 7. Payment Terms
Specify how you accept payment (bank transfer, credit card, PayPal) and any late payment policies.

### 8. Notes (Optional)
A brief thank-you note or payment instructions can improve the client relationship and reduce friction.

## Common Invoice Mistakes to Avoid

- **Missing due dates** — if you don't specify when payment is due, clients will pay whenever they feel like it
- **Vague descriptions** — leads to "what is this charge for?" emails
- **Wrong client details** — causes delays in corporate payment processes
- **No invoice number** — makes tracking impossible for both parties
- **Inconsistent formatting** — looks unprofessional
